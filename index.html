Research Interests

Distributed autonomy for aerial systems that must coordinate with imperfect information.

My work focuses on the interface between swarm control, communication constraints, distributed perception, and deployable aerial robotics systems.

Area 01

Distributed UAV Autonomy

Coordination algorithms and autonomy frameworks for multi-UAV teams operating without centralized assumptions.

Area 02

Communication-Aware Swarm Robotics

Swarm behavior that adapts to bandwidth limits, latency, packet loss, and incomplete network topology.

Area 03

Robust Aerial Robotics Systems

Field-oriented system design for reliable autonomy under sensing, compute, and environmental uncertainty.

Area 04

Distributed Perception–Communication Architecture

DPCA as a framework for connecting perception, message passing, and downstream swarm decision-making.

Current Research

Building a research bridge from simulation logic to experimental UAV platforms.

The current research direction studies how UAV teams maintain coordination quality when communication becomes limited, local, delayed, or partially unavailable.

Core framework

Communication-aware swarm autonomy for UAV teams

The research investigates distributed decision-making, neighbor-aware coordination, and perception-communication coupling. The goal is to define a framework that remains useful beyond idealized simulation settings and can support experimental validation.

Problem context

UAV swarms often assume stable communication, but real deployment environments produce intermittent links and uneven information access.

Method direction

Integrate communication quality into the architecture of perception, coordination, and local decision-making.

Validation goal

Connect algorithmic design with a practical UAV experimental platform for controlled field testing.
